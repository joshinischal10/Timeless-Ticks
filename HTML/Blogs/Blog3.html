A Watch Reimagined

From analog to digital, here's a deeper view on the watch aesthetic througthout the years

Date: Jan 14, 2025

It’s very clear that over the centuries, watches have drastically improved and changed. This can be seen in the watch designs as well. The journey of watchmaking is a story of progress. It has moved from the timeless beauty of early watches to the modern and stylish designs we see today.

The earliest watches were often worn as pendants or attached to clothing, serving as portable clocks. Those watches were said to be luxury items which were afforded only by the wealthy. The design focused much on the beauty of the watch with the cases made from gold and silver. Later on, using glass to protect the dial became standard, enhancing functionality and durability. Many brands like Waltham in the United States began producing watches on a large scale, making it more affordable for the common people.

During World War I for practicality on the battlefield, soldiers found it more convenient to glance at their wrists rather than a pocket watch. This lead to an increase in demand for wristwatches with Rolex and Omega being some leading brands. The mid-20th century was a golden age for watch design. The early centuries were characterised by elegance and innovation which kept on increasing and growing through the years.

Finally, the watch industry we know today is more diverse than ever thanks to the incredible innovations of the past. Overall, advancement in technology has lead to and will lead to even more durable, functional, and personal watches in the future. It may inspire new designs that push the boundaries of what watches can achieve.
